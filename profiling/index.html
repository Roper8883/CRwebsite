Profiling

Organise evidence, approvals, and final outputs without losing the thread.

Profiling inside CapstoneReady is designed to make record handling, evidence, approvals, and readiness clearer. It helps apprentices and employers work through the flow without pretending to replace official training or licensing systems.

Profiling is treated as a real workflow, not a loose notes area

The record, evidence, approval, and dispatch pieces are meant to stay connected all the way through the workflow.

What profiling means here

Profile cards, evidence, milestones, approvals, and outputs are part of the same operational story.

The product supports profiling as a structured workflow: prepare the record, attach relevant supporting material, route the approval steps where used, and retain a clear trail into the final output.

Records

Keep the card attached to the actual job context

Profile records are treated as structured workflow items rather than loose text notes or one-off screenshots.

Evidence

Attach photos, documents, and supporting material carefully

Evidence can be part of the workflow, but the user remains responsible for confidentiality, consent, and lawful upload rights.

Approvals

Support employer-linked review and final output flow

Where the workflow includes employer-linked review, the app helps organise that step rather than forcing a separate disconnected process.

Workflow structure

Prepare, review, approve, finalise, and dispatch without bouncing through scattered pages.

The current product direction already includes profile preparation, employer-linked review, signatures, finalisation, and PDF/output pathways. The website now explains that as one continuous workflow.

Approvals and signatures

Employer-linked steps stay visible

Employer pairing and approval support are designed to reduce ambiguity about who needs to do what next.

PDFs and outputs

Final outputs remain part of the same trail

Where PDFs or related outputs are generated or dispatched, the legal/support links and workflow context stay connected to the record.

Trustworthy progress

Formal tracking draws from completed records

Profiling-related progress views are built around completed record states rather than inflating readiness with drafts.

Profiling helps organise the path, not replace the official system

Training providers, regulators, licensing bodies, and employers still control the official decisions. The app supports record handling and readiness clarity around them.
