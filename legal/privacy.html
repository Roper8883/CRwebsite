Scope and product context

This privacy policy applies to the public website at https://capstoneready.com.au/, the shared Sparky's Ready platform, and the app family: Capstone Ready, AM2 Ready, Journeyman Ready, Red Seal Ready, EWRB Ready, SOLAS Ready, and Sparky's Pro.

Capstone Ready is the Australia-facing flagship on this domain. The wider family includes region-specific sister products for the United Kingdom, the United States, Canada, New Zealand, Ireland, and professional global workflows.

The products are training, revision, readiness, profiling, and workplace-support tools. They are not themselves a guarantee of qualification, licensing, employment, or provider acceptance.

What information we may collect

  • Account details such as email address, sign-in method, display name, and basic profile information.
  • Learning activity such as module progress, quiz attempts, review history, preferences, and progress state.
  • Profiling records, evidence attachments, workflow state, approvals, signatures, and related metadata where those features are used.
  • Support communications, screenshots, attachments, and issue details you choose to send us.
  • Purchase and entitlement information where premium features are available.
  • Approximate region or coarse location where the app needs it to select the right country pathway.
  • Privacy request details such as account identifiers, request type, and verification context when you ask for access, correction, export, or deletion.
  • Technical, device, and diagnostic information reasonably needed to operate, secure, and support the apps.
  • Technical request, hosting, security, and delivery metadata generated when the public website is loaded.

How information is collected and used

  • Directly from you when you create an account, sign in, complete profiling steps, upload evidence, purchase premium access, or contact support.
  • Automatically through the app and website when they need to store progress, maintain sessions, protect security, or deliver features.
  • To operate sign-in, profiling, learning, quiz, progress, support, and readiness workflows.
  • To generate and maintain records, outputs, and workflow state tied to the features you choose to use.
  • To investigate issues, protect the service, respond to support or privacy requests, and maintain lawful records of account or billing events.
  • To maintain App Store privacy-disclosure consistency for data types linked to app functionality, not third-party advertising or tracking.

Sign-in, app services, and infrastructure

Current implementation notes for this release show Apple-first sign-in, optional passkeys for returning users, and recovery-email support for older or recovery flows.

The repo and deployment notes also indicate that third-party services may support authentication, storage, secure backend workflows, notifications, purchase validation, and public-site hosting.

The public website is currently documented in the repo as Bluehost-hosted. Depending on live production configuration, additional delivery, DNS, CDN, or security layers may also sit in front of the site.

Profiling records, evidence, and uploads

If you upload or capture photos, documents, certifications, profile evidence, or other attachments, those items may be stored as part of your account and used in the relevant workflow you initiate.

You are responsible for ensuring you have the right to upload that material and that doing so does not breach confidentiality, privacy, workplace, employer, customer, training-provider, or legal restrictions.

Where the product supports profile review, approval, or dispatch workflows, information may be shown to the parties involved in that workflow.

Sharing, disclosure, and overseas handling

We do not describe this public build as selling personal information, sharing personal information for cross-context behavioural advertising, or using app data for third-party tracking.

Information may be handled by service providers or infrastructure partners used to operate authentication, hosting, storage, support, notifications, billing, or secure workflow services.

Depending on the services in use, information may be processed outside Australia. We take reasonable steps to use service arrangements appropriate to the feature being operated.

We may also disclose information where reasonably required by law, to protect legal rights, to investigate abuse or security issues, or to support the workflow a user initiates inside the product.

Retention, deletion, and security

We keep information only for as long as reasonably needed to operate the service, support your account, maintain expected workflow records, meet legal obligations, or protect security and fraud-prevention interests.

Current backend privacy-ops flows support export and delete request handling. A delete request may be queued first and may not immediately erase records that need to be retained for lawful security or recordkeeping reasons.

We take reasonable steps to secure app and account data, but no online or device-based system can promise absolute security.

Regional privacy rights and App Store disclosures

This app family is built for Australia, the United Kingdom, the United States, Canada, New Zealand, Ireland, and global professional workflows. Depending on where you live, you may have rights to be informed about collection, request access, request correction, request deletion, object or opt out of certain handling, complain to a regulator, or ask for a portable copy of information you provided.

Australian users can use the support route for APP-style access and correction requests. United Kingdom users can use the support route for UK GDPR-style access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, portability, and complaint support. United States users, including California residents where applicable law applies, can use the support route for know/access, delete, correct, opt-out, and non-discrimination enquiries. Canadian, New Zealand, and Irish users can also use support for local access, correction, deletion, and complaint pathways where applicable.

The app privacy manifest and App Store privacy answers should stay aligned with this policy: data used for app functionality, support, account state, profile/evidence workflows, purchase entitlement, and diagnostics must not be represented as advertising tracking unless the product changes.

Access, correction, deletion, complaints, and contact

You can use the in-app account-deletion path where it is available, and you can also contact support for data access, correction, export, deletion, or privacy enquiries.

Use the dedicated Account Deletion & Data Rights page for the practical request path.

If a law requires identity verification before we act on a request, we may ask for enough information to verify the account or request without asking for unnecessary extra data.
